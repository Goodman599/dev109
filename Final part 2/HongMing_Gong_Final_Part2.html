1. What was your previous knowledge of HTML/CSS/Javascript?

Previously, I had a vague idea of how to use HTML and CSS. I had taken a web development course a few years ago and just finished CSS, but I had forgotten a lot by this course.


2. Did you use a template or did you build your portfolio from scratch?

I built my portfolio up from scratch. I tried using one but the templates provided felt a bit too old for my taste.


3. What was your most challenging part of your portfolio (ie, the template, artifacts, CSS, etc.)?

The most challenging part was the CSS. It was hard to get everything to look right, and sometimes there would be weird bugs that took some time to fix.


4. How taking this class changed your view on Web Development?

I've realized that web development takes a lot of effort especially for usability and accessibility, and it has made me respect large websites more.


5. What would you recommend to future students taking this class?

I would recommend to not shy away from asking questions, and also to experiment. Understanding a tag or line of code is very important so you can use it flexibly.
